# AstraZeneca Cell Therapy Presentation Style Guide

## Analysis Summary

This guide is derived from analysis of 17 AstraZeneca Cell Therapy (CTDO) presentations covering executive reports, supplier strategy, vendor management, courier logistics, and operational reviews.

**Corpus Statistics:**
- 17 presentations analyzed
- 204 total slides
- Average: 12 slides per presentation (range: 5-28)
- Average: 149 words per slide
- Average: 6.4 bullet points per slide
- 75 slides contain tables (37%)
- 8 presentations include charts

---

## 1. Presentation Structure Patterns

### Typical Slide Counts by Presentation Type

| Type | Slides | Description |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Executive Report | 5-6 | Board-level summary, focused and high-impact |
| Strategy Deck | 8-14 | Detailed analysis with supporting evidence |
| Comprehensive Review | 26-28 | Full operational/vendor review with appendices |
| Tactical/Assignment | 6-7 | Specific operational decisions with data tables |

### Standard Presentation Flow

Most presentations follow this pattern:

1. **Title Slide** (1 slide)
   - Minimal text (3-30 words)
   - Company identifier: "AstraZeneca" or "ASTRAZENECA | CELL THERAPY DEVELOPMENT & OPERATIONS"
   - Document title
   - Author/presenter name (optional)
   - Date and confidentiality marker

2. **Agenda/Overview** (1 slide, optional)
   - Numbered or bulleted list of topics
   - Example: "RFI/RFP Overview | Suppliers | Assumptions | Spend Projections | Recommendations | Next Steps"

3. **Executive Summary** (1 slide)
   - 100-220 words
   - Portfolio Health indicator (Poor/Fair/Good)
   - Key findings in narrative form
   - Quantified metrics (percentages, trends)

4. **Content Slides** (3-20 slides)
   - Data tables, charts, analysis
   - Average 150-250 words per slide
   - Mix of narrative text and structured data

5. **Recommendations** (1-2 slides)
   - Numbered action items
   - Owner, timeline, priority for each
   - Clear justification

6. **Next Steps** (1 slide)
   - Action items with owners
   - Timeline (quarters or specific dates)
   - Often formatted as numbered list

7. **Q&A/Discussion** (optional)
   - Minimal text, just "Questions?" or "Discussion"

## 4. Professional Language Patterns

### Header/Title Conventions

Use clear, action-oriented headers:

**Good Examples:**
- "Executive Summary"
- "Strategic Recommendations"
- "Risk Assessment"
- "Network Performance Trend"
- "Cost Improvement Priority Matrix"
- "Recommended Next Steps"
- "Where We Stand Today"
- "The Bottom Line"

**Section Header Patterns:**
- "[Topic] — [Subtitle]" (e.g., "Executive Summary — Key Findings")
- "[Phase X] OVERVIEW" (e.g., "PHASE 2 OVERVIEW")
- "[Topic] · [Qualifier]" (e.g., "BLA #1 · WHERE WE ARE")

### Strategic Language

Use decisive, forward-looking language:

- "Establish a centralized vendor management model with clear roles, responsibilities, and measurable KPIs"
- "Digital CoI/CoC and parallel doc prep are priority kaizen targets"
- "Building the ecosystem that will serve 90,000 patients annually"
- "Every supplier interaction should ultimately shorten cycle times, reduce complexity, and improve the experience of receiving this therapy"

### Risk/Issue Language

Be direct about problems without being alarmist:

- "The CGT logistics program is in critical condition with Marken showing severely deteriorating performance"
- "Single-source dependency creates program continuity risk"
- "Current oversight insufficient given Critical risk level"
- "Volume scaling stress revealed process failures"
- "This requires validation before declaring success"

### Recommendation Language

Be specific and actionable:

- "Negotiate explicit CPI cap (recommend 3-4%) into contract before execution"
- "Renegotiate fuel sharing from 75/25 to 50/50 to align with World Courier terms"
- "Use QuickSTAT pricing framework as template for standardization"
- "Close Phase II Task-1 SKUs on Q3 2027 timeline"
- "Maintain Tier-1 SRM cadence (monthly + biannual exec)"

### Quantified Statements

Always include specific metrics:

- "Incident rate surged from 0% to 8.3% month-over-month"
- "4 patient safety incidents represent 40% of total failures"
- "On-time delivery improving from 20% to 66.7%"
- "Shipment volume grew 42% (45 to 64)"
- "Biocair accounts for 62% of all incidents (80 of 130)"
- "12x volume increase (10 to 120 shipments)"

## 8. Slide Design Principles

### Text Density

- Title slides: Very sparse (3-30 words)
- Summary slides: Moderate (100-220 words)
- Content slides: Moderate-heavy (150-250 words)
- Table slides: Variable (100-500 words)

### Bullet Point Guidelines

- Average 6-7 bullets per slide
- Mix of short phrases and complete sentences
- Use numbered lists for sequential items
- Use bullet lists for non-sequential items

### Visual Hierarchy

1. **Header** (large, bold)
2. **Subheader** (medium, often separated by em-dash or pipe)
3. **Body text** (regular weight)
4. **Supporting detail** (smaller or in tables)

### Formatting Conventions

- Use em-dashes (—) for separating elements
- Use pipes (|) in headers for organization hierarchy
- Use dots/bullets (·) for inline lists
- Use parentheticals for clarification: "(trend: improving)"
- Include dates in format: "May 2026" or "Q3 2027"

## 9. Executive Summary Template

The ideal executive summary includes:

```
[Portfolio Health: Poor/Fair/Good]

[Opening statement: 1-2 sentences summarizing current state]

[Key metric 1 with specific numbers and trend]
[Key metric 2 with specific numbers and trend]
[Key metric 3 with specific numbers and trend]

[Concluding statement: 1-2 sentences on implications or urgent concerns]
```

**Example:**
> **Executive Summary**
> 
> **Portfolio Health: Fair**
> 
> The CGT logistics program maintains a perfect patient safety record with zero safety or product integrity incidents across 130 total incidents. However, Q1 2026 performance reveals a concerning divergence: while May 2026 shows a dramatically improved incident rate (11.1% vs. 40%+ in March-April), on-time delivery has deteriorated from 85% in January to 75% in April—a 10 percentage point decline that directly threatens patient therapy windows.

## 10. Recommendation Slide Template

Structure recommendations as numbered items with:

1. **Action Title** (bold)
2. **Category and Timeline** (e.g., "Technology | Q4 2026 - Q2 2027")
3. **Justification** (1-2 sentences)

**Example:**
> **Strategic Recommendations**
>
> 1. **Dual-source logistics strategy for all CGT products**  
>    Supplier Diversification | Q3-Q4 2026  
>    Current Marken dependency creates single point of failure; diversification enables competitive pressure.
>
> 2. **Implement predictive logistics platform with AI-driven disruption forecasting**  
>    Technology | Q4 2026 - Q2 2027  
>    Reactive incident management insufficient for CGT; need predictive capability to intervene before patient impact.

## 11. Next Steps Template

Format as numbered or bulleted list with:
- Clear action item
- Owner in parentheses or separate column
- Target date/quarter

**Example:**
> **Next Steps**
>
> 1. Initiate Dual-Source Depot Services RFP (David Oliver / Dan Hariga) — Q2 2026
> 2. Update base cost/patient for US, Ex-US, EU/UK lanes (David Oliver / Trishala) — Q2 2026
> 3. Implement intake slot scheduling at Tarzana & Rockville to resolve 2-hr dock wait — Q3 2026

## 12. Tone and Voice Guidelines

### Do:
- Be direct and confident
- Lead with the most important information
- Use specific numbers and percentages
- Acknowledge problems while proposing solutions
- Use active voice
- Include clear timelines and owners

### Don't:
- Use vague language ("significant improvement")
- Bury the lead in excessive context
- Present problems without solutions
- Use passive voice excessively
- Leave actions without owners or deadlines
- Overuse jargon without context

### Example Transformations:

**Before:** "There have been some issues with performance lately."  
**After:** "On-time delivery declined 10 percentage points (85% to 75%) from January to April 2026."

**Before:** "We should probably look at other options."  
**After:** "Recommend dual-sourcing strategy: qualify QuickSTAT as backup courier by Q3 2026."

**Before:** "The project is going well."  
**After:** "Project tracking to plan: 6 of 8 milestones complete; on target for Q4 2026 BLA submission."

For each slide, verify:

- [ ] Clear, descriptive header (not generic)

- [ ] Slide number visible
- [ ] Content supports one main message
- [ ] Metrics are specific and dated
- [ ] Acronyms are industry-standard or defined
- [ ] Actions have owners and timelines
- [ ] Visual hierarchy is clear
